# Project Context

## Vision

RealFarm makes real agriculture remotely accessible through a game-like interface. The platform must remain honest about biological time, operational constraints, and agricultural risk.

## Primary user

The primary user is a customer/player who has enough disposable income to pay for a guided farming experience and wants real produce without owning land or performing daily physical labor.

## Core scenario

1. The player browses available real plots.
2. The player leases a plot for a defined period or crop cycle.
3. The player selects a crop from an agronomically valid catalog.
4. The platform creates a crop cycle and assigns the real plot.
5. The player follows progress in a 2D digital farm.
6. Sensors and farm staff maintain the crop.
7. The player may submit allowed action requests.
8. The system either executes safely, schedules a work order, or rejects the request with a reason.
9. The farm records evidence and care history.
10. The accepted harvest belongs to the player according to the lease policy.
11. The player receives the produce through pickup or delivery.

## Product positioning

RealFarm is not:

- a purely virtual game;
- an unrestricted remote-control system;
- a guarantee that biology will always produce a fixed yield;
- a replacement for professional agronomic decisions.

It is a managed real-farming experience with a game interface.

## Graduation-project contribution

The strongest technical contribution is the orchestration between digital player intent and real-world execution:

- Digital Twin interaction;
- agronomic policy engine;
- IoT automation;
- human work-order scheduling;
- evidence timeline;
- harvest entitlement and traceability.

AI and blockchain support the product but are not the product's core identity.
